# LTV Maximizer — Reference
**Source: $100M Offers + ACQ LTV Framework — Alex Hormozi**

> "The customer who already bought is 5–10x less expensive to sell than a stranger."
> "Before asking 'how do I get more customers?', ask 'how do I make each customer worth more?'"

---

## LTV Formula by Model

### One-Time / Project-Based
```
LTV = Average Contract Value × Gross Margin × (1 + Repeat Purchase Rate)
```

### Subscription / SaaS
```
LTV = (Monthly Revenue per Customer × Gross Margin %) ÷ Monthly Churn Rate
```

### Coaching / High-Ticket Program
```
LTV = Program Revenue × Gross Margin + (Upsell Revenue × Upsell Rate) + (Continuity × Avg Months)
```

## The 5 LTV Levers — Complete Guide

### Lever 1 — Raise Prices

**Impact:** +20% price = +20% revenue per customer with no new work.

**When you're undercharging:**
- Close rate > 50% consistently (market is saying yes too easily)
- Clients frequently say "that's all?" or "wow, that's cheap"
- Your best clients would happily pay more (ask them)
- ROI for clients is > 10:1

**How to raise prices:**
1. Add a new deliverable to the offer stack (justifies the increase)
2. Document the new value stack with standalone pricing
3. Create new price anchor
4. Grandfather existing clients
5. Launch new price to all new prospects

**Price raise sequence:**
```
Current price: $3,000
Add 1 new high-value element: e.g., bi-weekly group call ($2,400 standalone value)
New value stack: $24,900 → New price: $5,000 → Close rate drops from 50% to 35% → Revenue per close goes up 67%
```

---

### Lever 2 — Reduce Churn

**Impact:** Halving churn doubles LTV — without any new customers.

**Churn happens for 5 reasons (in order of frequency):**

| Reason | Diagnosis | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| No AHA moment | Client never got first result | Add Week 1 quick win |
| Poor onboarding | Client didn't know how to use what they bought | Redesign onboarding sequence |
| Value not visible | Client can't see the ROI | Add reporting / results dashboard |
| Better alternative found | Competitor stole them | Build switching costs (community, integrations, data) |
| Life circumstances | Budget cut, company change | Pause vs. cancel option; annual billing |

**The Onboarding Fix (handles 60–70% of churn):**
```
Day 1:  Welcome + expectations set + first action assigned
Day 3:  Check-in (did they take the first action?)
Day 7:  First result milestone (the AHA moment)
Day 14: Progress check + next milestone set
Day 30: Review + case study opportunity
```

**Annual billing impact on churn:**
- Monthly billing at 10% churn = 5-month average lifespan
- Annual billing at same "true churn" = effective monthly churn drops to ~3% (locked in for 12 months)
- Impact: LTV nearly doubles with same product, same price, same quality

---

### Lever 3 — Increase Purchase Frequency

**Impact:** If average customer buys 2× instead of 1×, LTV doubles.

**The Ascension Ladder:**
Design a clear "what's next" for every completed customer.

```
Entry Offer → Core Offer → Continuity → Premium Tier → Private Advisory
```

**Upsell timing:**
- Best upsell window: immediately after a win (when belief is highest)
- Second best: at end of current program (natural transition)
- Never: in the middle of a struggle or when client is frustrated

**Upsell script:**
```
"You just achieved [result]. The next natural step is [next offer].
Here's what [3 people exactly like you] did next — and what happened."
CTA: "Want to talk about whether [next offer] makes sense for you?"
```

---

### Lever 4 — Increase Average Order Value (AOV)

**Impact:** AOV × same volume = proportional revenue increase.

**3 AOV tactics:**

**Tactic 1 — Order Bump (at point of sale)**
- Offer one additional item at checkout
- Price: 20–40% of core offer price
- Example: Core offer $2,000 → "Add the [templates pack] for $397 more"
- Typical attach rate: 15–30%

**Tactic 2 — Upsell After Purchase**
- Immediately after payment: offer a higher-tier version or add-on
- Example: "Since you just enrolled in X, you qualify for a 1-on-1 implementation call for $497"
- Typical attach rate: 10–20%

**Tactic 3 — Bundle**
- Offer multiple products together at a discount vs. individual prices
- Bundle price = 70–80% of combined individual prices
- Increases perceived value while increasing AOV

---

### Lever 5 — Extend Relationship Duration

**Impact:** Every additional month of retention = full-margin revenue with zero CAC.

**5 Relationship Extension Tactics:**

**Tactic 1 — Annual Billing**
Locks in commitment. Most powerful single tactic.
Offer: "Pay annually and get 2 months free" (16.7% discount, 100% commitment).

**Tactic 2 — Community**
Social capital is a switching cost. The longer they're in the community, the higher the cost to leave.
Investment: 1–2 hours/week of founder/team presence.

**Tactic 3 — Data + Integrations**
The more their data lives in your platform, the higher the switching cost.
(SaaS specific) Build integrations that make migration painful.

**Tactic 4 — Results Compounding**
Design the program so results compound over time (not all delivered upfront).
Month 1: Lead gen system built → Month 3: First consistent leads → Month 6: Paid ads profitable
Each milestone extends the relationship.

**Tactic 5 — The "What's Next" Offer**
Always have a clear next tier ready before the current program ends.
Transition clients proactively — never let the relationship end without a next conversation.
