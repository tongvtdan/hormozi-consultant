# Pricing Architecture — Reference
**Source: $100M Offers + ACQ Pricing Framework — Alex Hormozi**

> "Price communicates value. If you underprice, you signal low quality. If you overprice without proof, you signal risk."
> "Charge what the transformation is worth. Not what the labor costs."

---

## The Pricing Hierarchy

```
1. Position correctly (ICP + Dream outcome + Proof)
2. Set the value anchor (standalone value >> price)
3. Design the offer stack (what's included + standalone values)
4. Choose the price point (10x gap minimum)
5. Select the payment structure (one-time / monthly / annual / payment plan)
6. Design the guarantee (removes last barrier)
```

**Shortcut that kills businesses:** Skip straight to step 5 without completing steps 1–4.

## The 4-Tier Pricing Ladder

### Tier 1 — Lead Magnet ($0)

**Purpose:** Convert strangers into contacts (email, DM, phone)
**Price:** Free (requires time investment)
**Rules:**
- Must solve a narrow, specific problem completely
- Must reveal a larger problem that your Core Offer solves
- Grand Slam quality: "would they pay $50 for this?"

**Examples:**
- B2B: "The 23-Point Agency Lead Audit" (PDF or tool)
- Consumer: "The 7-Day Macro Reset Guide" (mini-course)
- SaaS: "Free plan with 3 key use cases"

---

### Tier 2 — Entry Offer (Low Friction)

**Purpose:** Break the non-buyer identity; start the buying relationship
**Price:** $1–$297 (consumer) | $500–$2,000 (B2B)

**Why it matters:**
The first transaction is psychologically the hardest. Lower friction = more buyers.
A customer who paid $97 is 10x more likely to buy a $3,000 offer than a non-buyer.

**Design rules:**
- Deliver a quick win (result within 24–72 hours)
- Must stand alone as valuable (not feel like a gimme)
- Should naturally reveal the need for the Core Offer

**Pricing examples:**

| Niche | Entry Offer | Price |
|---|---|---|
| B2B agency | "1-Day Funnel Audit + Report" | $500 |
| Coaching | "90-Minute Strategy Call + Action Plan" | $197 |
| SaaS | "Starter plan with core features" | $29–$99/month |
| eCommerce | "Sampler box" or "First month" | $19–$49 |

---

### Tier 3 — Core Offer (Primary Revenue Driver)

**Purpose:** Deliver the primary transformation; primary source of revenue
**Price:** $500–$5,000 (consumer) | $3,000–$100,000+ (B2B)

**Pricing anchor formula:**
1. List all deliverables in the offer
2. Assign standalone market value to each (use delivery-cube.md rates)
3. Sum = Value Anchor
4. Reveal price = Value Anchor ÷ 5 or more

**Example value stack:**

| Deliverable | Standalone Value |
|---|---|
| 3-month DFY lead gen (12 weeks) | $12,000 |
| Custom sales script | $2,500 |
| Weekly strategy call (12 × 1 hr) | $6,000 |
| Playbook library (industry-specific) | $2,000 |
| Community access (annual) | $2,400 |
| **Total standalone value** | **$24,900** |

**Price:** $6,000 → Gap: 4.15:1 (add more value to hit 5:1)

---

### Tier 4 — Continuity / Ascension

**Purpose:** Extend relationship, extend revenue, extend results
**Price:** Monthly recurring or step-up to higher tier

**3 Continuity Models:**

| Type | Description | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| Subscription continuation | Monthly fee to maintain result | SaaS, membership, retainer |
| Mastermind / community | Higher-tier group for advanced clients | Coaching, B2B, consulting |
| Next-level offer | More intensive version of core | When clients want "more of the same but bigger" |

**Ascension path example:**
```
Lead Magnet ($0) → Entry Workshop ($297) → Core Program ($3,000) → Mastermind ($1,000/mo) → Private Advisory ($5,000/mo)
```

## Payment Structure Options

### One-Time Payment
- Highest conversion friction
- Best for lower-priced offers or confident buyers
- Maximizes upfront cash flow

### Monthly Subscription
- Lowest barrier to start
- Risk: easy to cancel
- Best when ongoing value is clearly delivered each month

### Annual Subscription
- 2x–4x better retention than monthly
- Best offer: 2 months free (16.7% discount) or flat 20% off
- One year of commitment = higher investment = higher action = better results = lower churn

### Payment Plan (installments)
- Makes high-ticket accessible without lowering price
- Standard: 3–6 payments; total is 20–30% higher than one-time price
- Example: $3,000 one-time OR 3 × $1,197 ($591 premium for plan)

### Performance-Based
- Pay only when results are achieved (e.g., "Pay X% of revenue generated")
- Highest trust signal; eliminates price objection
- Risk: requires confidence in delivery AND measurability of results

## Raising Prices (The Most Underused Lever)

### When to raise prices
- When close rate is above 50% (you're undercharging)
- When demand consistently exceeds capacity
- When adding proof (every strong case study justifies a price increase)
- When results data shows ROI > 10:1 for clients

### How to raise prices without losing clients
1. Grandfather existing clients at current rate
2. Announce to new prospects at new rate
3. Add a deliverable to justify the increase
4. Document the new value stack with the new price anchor

### Pricing psychology principles

| Principle | Application |
|---|---|
| **Price anchoring** | Show $24,900 value before $6,000 price |
| **Odd number pricing** | $4,997 feels more specific than $5,000 |
| **Three-tier pricing** | Good / Better / Best; most choose middle |
| **Annual vs. monthly framing** | "$1,200/year" vs. "$100/month" — same price, different anchor |
| **Loss framing** | "What does one more year of this problem cost you?" |
| **Social proof at price point** | "Our clients at this tier average [result]" |

## Pricing by Business Model

### Agency / DFY Service
**Recommended:** Project-based or retainer + performance kicker
- Project: one-time fee for defined scope
- Retainer: monthly with performance bonus (aligns incentives)

**Price formula:** (Hours × desired hourly rate) × 3 (for business overhead, profit, and value buffer)

### High-Ticket Coaching
**Recommended:** Fixed program price (not hourly)
**Standard range:** $3,000–$25,000 for 90-day to 12-month programs
**Gate to raise price:** ≥10 identical-ICP case studies with quantified results

### SaaS
**Recommended:** Freemium or trial → Monthly → Annual upgrade
**Pricing tiers:** 3 tiers (starter / professional / enterprise)
**Expansion revenue:** The key to 100%+ NRR

### Course / Info Product
**Recommended:** One-time purchase + upsell to community or coaching
**Price anchors:**
- DIY course: $197–$997
- Group coaching: $1,000–$5,000
- High-touch mastermind: $5,000–$50,000
