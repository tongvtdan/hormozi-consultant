# LTV:CAC Models — Reference
**Source: $100M Offers + ACQ Scaling Framework — Alex Hormozi**

> "LTV:CAC is the only number that tells you if you have permission to scale."
> "3:1 is the minimum. 5:1 is healthy. 10:1 means you're leaving money on the table — raise prices."

---

## The Core Formula

```
LTV:CAC Ratio = Customer Lifetime Value ÷ Customer Acquisition Cost

LTV  = Average Revenue Per Customer × Gross Margin % × Average Customer Lifespan
CAC  = Total Sales & Marketing Spend ÷ Number of New Customers Acquired
```

---

## LTV Calculation Models by Business Type

### Model 1 — One-Time Purchase (Agency / Consulting / Done-For-You)

```
LTV = Average Contract Value × Gross Margin % × (1 + Repeat Purchase Rate)
```

**Example:**
- Average contract: $8,000
- Gross margin: 65%
- 30% of clients buy a second project within 12 months

```
LTV = $8,000 × 0.65 × 1.30 = $6,760
```

**To increase LTV:** add retainer component, build continuity offer, increase contract value.

---

### Model 2 — Subscription / SaaS / Membership

```
LTV = (Monthly Revenue per Customer × Gross Margin %) ÷ Monthly Churn Rate
```

**Example:**
- Monthly subscription: $500/month
- Gross margin: 80%
- Monthly churn: 5% (20-month average lifespan)

```
LTV = ($500 × 0.80) ÷ 0.05 = $8,000
```

**Churn sensitivity — same subscription at different churn rates:**

| Monthly Churn | Avg. Lifespan | LTV (at $500/mo, 80% GM) |
|---|---|---|
| 2% | 50 months | $20,000 |
| 5% | 20 months | $8,000 |
| 10% | 10 months | $4,000 |
| 15% | 6.7 months | $2,667 |

**Key insight:** Cutting churn from 10% to 5% doubles LTV — without acquiring a single new customer.

---

### Model 3 — High-Ticket Coaching / Group Programs

```
LTV = (Program Price × Gross Margin) + (Upsell Revenue × Upsell Rate) + (Continuity Value)
```

**Example:**
- Program price: $6,000 at 75% margin = $4,500
- 40% of clients buy a $3,000 mastermind = $1,200 expected value
- 25% continue on a $500/month continuity for 12 months = $1,500 expected value

```
LTV = $4,500 + $1,200 + $1,500 = $7,200
```

## CAC Calculation Models

### Full CAC (Blended)

```
CAC = (Total Marketing Spend + Total Sales Payroll) ÷ New Customers Acquired
```

**Include in marketing spend:**
- Ad spend (all platforms)
- Content creation costs
- SEO / organic tools
- Lead magnet production
- Affiliate commissions

**Include in sales payroll:**
- Setter salaries/commissions
- Closer salaries/commissions
- Sales manager overhead

**Example:**
- Monthly ad spend: $10,000
- Content + tools: $2,000
- Sales team payroll: $8,000
- Total: $20,000
- New customers: 10

```
CAC = $20,000 ÷ 10 = $2,000
```

With LTV of $7,200 → LTV:CAC = 3.6:1 ✅

### Channel-Specific CAC

Track CAC per channel to find highest-ROI source:

| Channel | Monthly Spend | Customers | CAC | LTV:CAC |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Warm outreach | $0 (time only) | 3 | ~$500 (time-adjusted) | 14.4:1 ✅✅ |
| Content machine | $500/mo (tools) | 4 | $125 | 57.6:1 ✅✅ |
| Cold outreach | $2,000 (SDR) | 3 | $667 | 10.8:1 ✅✅ |
| Paid ads | $10,000 | 5 | $2,000 | 3.6:1 ✅ |

**Optimization insight:** Scale channels with the best LTV:CAC first. Warm and content almost always win — which is why they come before paid.

## The LTV:CAC Action Table

| Ratio | Status | Action Required |
|---|---|---|
| < 1:1 | Losing money | Stop paid spend. Fix offer pricing or cut CAC immediately. |
| 1:1–2:1 | Breaking even | Not safe to scale. Diagnose: is LTV too low or CAC too high? |
| 2:1–2.9:1 | Getting close | Improve one lever before activating paid scale. |
| 3:1 | Minimum viable | Green light for paid ads. Monitor tightly. |
| 4:1–5:1 | Healthy | Scale paid ads. Consider testing new channels. |
| 5:1–9:1 | Very strong | Aggressive scale authorized. |
| 10:1+ | Exceptional | Almost certainly underpriced. Raise prices. |

## LTV Improvement Levers

### Lever 1 — Raise Average Contract Value
- Niche down (same framework, more specific ICP = higher prices)
- Add premium delivery tier (DFY > DWY > DIY)
- Bundle more deliverables into a value stack

### Lever 2 — Reduce Churn (subscriptions)
- Improve onboarding (most churn happens in month 1)
- Add a quick win in the first 7 days
- Create switching costs (integrations, community, data)
- Add a continuity offer to one-time buyers

### Lever 3 — Increase Purchase Frequency
- Design a continuity offer that solves the "next problem"
- Build a natural upsell ladder from low-ticket to high-ticket
- Create community or mastermind that extends the relationship

### Lever 4 — Improve Gross Margin
- Raise prices (most underused lever for service businesses)
- Productize delivery (reduce time-per-client)
- Automate fulfillment where judgment isn't required
- Reduce cost of tools / contractors / overhead

---

## CAC Reduction Levers

### Lever 1 — Increase Organic Lead Volume
- Content machine reduces paid dependency
- Warm referrals have near-zero CAC
- SEO/YouTube compound over time

### Lever 2 — Improve Close Rate
- Better qualified leads (stronger ICP targeting)
- Better sales process (script, objection handling, follow-up)
- Better proof (testimonials, case studies, guarantees)

### Lever 3 — Improve Show Rate
- Better calendar confirmation sequences
- Shorter time-to-call (book within 24 hours of opt-in)
- Increase personal touchpoints between opt-in and call

### Lever 4 — Reduce Ad Waste
- Tighter audience targeting
- Kill underperforming ad sets faster (at 500 impressions)
- Improve lead magnet quality to increase opt-in rate

## The Payback Period

```
CAC Payback Period = CAC ÷ (Monthly Revenue per Customer × Gross Margin %)
```

**Target:** < 6 months for most service businesses
**Warning:** > 12 months = cash flow risk at scale

**Example:**
- CAC: $2,000
- Monthly revenue: $500
- Gross margin: 80%

```
Payback = $2,000 ÷ ($500 × 0.80) = $2,000 ÷ $400 = 5 months ✅
```

A 5-month payback at 20-month average lifespan = 15 months of pure profit per customer after payback.
