# Hiring Blueprint — Reference
**Source: ACQ Hiring Framework — Acquisition.com**

> "Every hire should replace you in something. If it doesn't free your time or multiply your output, it's overhead."
> "Hire slowly. Fire quickly. Hire for who they are. Train for what they do."

---

## The Core Hiring Rule

**Hire to replace yourself in the task with the highest leverage ratio:**

```
Leverage Ratio = Your Hourly Rate ÷ Market Rate for That Task
```

A task you do at $500/hr that someone else can do at $50/hr has a 10:1 leverage ratio.
Hire that person first.

---

## The Value-Per-Hour Delegation Audit

**Step 1 — Time audit (track for 2 weeks):**
List every task completed, the time spent, and what that time is worth if you were doing the highest-leverage activity instead.

**Step 2 — Classify each task:**

| Category | Description | Action |
|---|---|---|
| **$10/hr tasks** | Admin, scheduling, data entry, inbox management | Hire VA immediately |
| **$50/hr tasks** | Fulfillment delivery, content editing, customer support | Hire specialist |
| **$150/hr tasks** | Sales calls, qualified lead follow-up | Hire setter/closer |
| **$500/hr tasks** | Strategy, offer design, high-value relationships | Keep with founder |
| **$1,000+/hr tasks** | Acquisitions, partnerships, board-level | Never delegate |

**Step 3 — Rank tasks by leverage ratio and hire in that order.**

## The ACQ First 4 Hires Sequence

For a service business founder at $10k–$500k/month:

### Hire 1 — Operations / Virtual Assistant

**Purpose:** Remove you from all $10/hr tasks
**Tasks they own:**
- Calendar management and scheduling
- Email triage and routing
- CRM data entry and follow-up reminders
- Administrative errands (documents, research, tracking)
- Meeting prep and notes

**Where to find:** Onlinejobs.ph, Belay, Time etc., LinkedIn
**Cost range:** $1,500–$5,000/month (offshore) | $4,000–$8,000/month (US)
**Hours freed per week:** 10–20 hours
**Your time value freed:** $2,000–$10,000/week

---

### Hire 2 — Fulfillment Specialist

**Purpose:** Remove you from delivery so you can focus on growth
**Tasks they own:**
- Client onboarding and kickoff
- Core service delivery (as trained by your SOP)
- Client communication and project management
- Quality check against your standards

**Gate criteria before hiring:**
- [ ] SOP for delivery is documented
- [ ] You can train them in ≤ 5 days
- [ ] Delivery quality can be measured objectively

**Where to find:** LinkedIn, industry-specific boards, referrals from clients
**Cost range:** $3,000–$8,000/month depending on skill level
**Hours freed per week:** 15–30 hours
**Your time value freed:** $3,000–$15,000/week

---

### Hire 3 — Setter / SDR (Sales Development Rep)

**Purpose:** Remove you from lead qualification and first-touch outreach
**Tasks they own:**
- Warm and cold outreach (following the setter script)
- Lead qualification (confirm pain + authority + budget)
- Calendar booking for closing calls
- Follow-up sequences (3–7 touches)

**Gate criteria before hiring:**
- [ ] Warm outreach script is proven (≥ 20% conversation-to-book rate)
- [ ] You have ≥ 5 paying customers to use as proof examples
- [ ] CRM is set up to track setter activity

**Where to find:** Breezy.hr, LinkedIn, sales communities, referrals
**Cost range:** $3,000–$5,000/month base + $200–$500/booked call
**Calls booked/month target:** 20–40
**Hours freed per week:** 10–15 hours

---

### Hire 4 — Closer / Account Executive

**Purpose:** Remove you from sales calls so you can focus on offers and strategy
**Tasks they own:**
- Discovery and diagnosis calls
- Presenting the offer
- Handling objections
- Collecting payment and onboarding

**Gate criteria before hiring:**
- [ ] You have closed ≥ 20 calls personally (know the script inside out)
- [ ] Close rate ≥ 25% on qualified calls
- [ ] Script, objection handling, and proof assets are documented
- [ ] You can track their close rate independently

**Where to find:** Refer to closers in your industry; LinkedIn; referrals from clients who became fans
**Cost range:** $3,000–$6,000/month base + 5–15% commission on closed revenue
**Hours freed per week:** 10–20 hours

---

### Hire 5 — Integrator / COO

**Purpose:** Remove you from management and operations
**This hire only makes sense when:**
- You have Hires 1–4 in place
- You spend > 30% of your time managing people
- The business can sustain their salary without strain

**Tasks they own:**
- Daily team management and accountability
- Process improvement and SOP maintenance
- Reporting (weekly metrics dashboard)
- Removing operational blockers
- Project management across departments

**Where to find:** Rocket Fuel (book) → Integrator profile → EOS Worldwide network
**Cost range:** $80,000–$180,000/year

## Hire Profile Template

Use this for every hire before opening a role:

```
HIRE PROFILE
========================
Role title:           [specific — not "marketing person"]
Replacing founder in: [specific tasks + hours/week freed]
Leverage ratio:       [your $/hr ÷ market rate = X:1]
Primary outcome owned: [one measurable metric]
Must-have criteria:   [3 non-negotiables — no exceptions]
Nice-to-have:         [skills you'd love but won't filter on]
Compensation:         [base + variable structure]
Reporting to:         [who]
90-day success looks like: [specific measurable outcomes]
Red flags to screen for: [3 deal-breakers in interview]
========================
```

## Hiring Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Why It Fails | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Hiring a "generalist" who does everything | No one does anything excellently | Hire for one specific outcome |
| Hiring before SOPs are built | They'll fail or do it wrong | Document the role before hiring |
| Hiring too fast because you're overwhelmed | Desperation hiring = bad hires | Hire slow; use contractors first |
| Hiring a friend or family member | Hard to fire; personal costs | Hire for outcomes, not relationship |
| Paying too much equity too early | Dilutes you before they've proven anything | Pay cash first; equity only for proven contributors |
| Not tracking their performance metrics | Can't tell if they're working | Set one primary metric per role from Day 1 |

## The 30-Day Onboarding Protocol

**Week 1 — Shadow and Learn**
- Observe how you do the role (or watch recorded SOPs)
- Learn the tools, platforms, and context
- No independent decisions yet

**Week 2 — Supervised Execution**
- Execute tasks with your oversight
- Daily 15-minute check-in
- Identify gaps in their knowledge

**Week 3 — Independent Execution with Review**
- Execute independently
- You review output daily
- Feedback loops tight

**Week 4 — Full Handoff**
- They own the outcomes
- Weekly check-in only
- Track primary metric weekly

**At 30 days:** If primary metric is not trending right, diagnose: training gap vs. skill gap vs. wrong hire.
