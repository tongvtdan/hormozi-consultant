# Scale Sequence — Reference
**Source: ACQ Scaling Framework — Acquisition.com**

> "More. Then Better. Then New. In that order. Every time."
> "The fastest way to kill a business is to add new complexity before mastering what's working."

---

## The Three-Phase Framework

### Phase 1 — MORE

**When you are here:** Volume is the primary constraint. The channel/offer works — you're just not doing enough of it.

**Signs you are in Phase 1:**
- Revenue is inconsistent week-to-week
- You haven't hit 10+ paying warm customers yet
- Primary lead channel is not running at full capacity
- Team is not executing the same winning playbook daily
- You are still testing whether the offer converts

**What to do:**
- Run the primary lead channel at maximum volume
- Do not change the offer until you have ≥10 reps at full volume
- Do not add a second channel
- Do not optimize conversion until volume is proven

**Trap to avoid:** "I should improve the copy first before I scale it." No. Volume produces the data needed to improve.

**Phase 1 graduation criteria:**
- [ ] ≥10 paying customers from primary channel
- [ ] Primary channel running at consistent weekly cadence
- [ ] Lead volume is predictable (variance < 20% week-to-week)
- [ ] Team executing identical playbook every week

---

### Phase 2 — BETTER

**When you are here:** Volume is consistent. Now quality, conversion, and unit economics are the constraint.

**Signs you are in Phase 2:**
- Lead volume is stable but close rate is below 30%
- Customer results have quality variance
- LTV:CAC ratio is below 3:1
- Refund rate or churn is above industry average
- Delivery capacity is maxed and quality is suffering

**What to do:**
- Optimize the conversion process (offer positioning, sales script, objection handling)
- Improve fulfillment quality (SOPs, delivery team training, client success touchpoints)
- Fix unit economics (raise prices, reduce CAC, extend LTV)
- Measure everything: track close rate, show rate, refund rate, NPS

**Trap to avoid:** Adding a second lead channel while quality is the constraint. It accelerates bad economics.

**Phase 2 graduation criteria:**
- [ ] Close rate on qualified calls ≥ 30%
- [ ] LTV:CAC ratio ≥ 3:1
- [ ] Customer NPS ≥ 50
- [ ] Churn / refund rate within industry norms
- [ ] Delivery is consistent and team can execute without founder

---

### Phase 3 — NEW

**When you are here:** Volume and quality are both maximized. Now expand.

**Signs you are in Phase 3:**
- Core channel is maxed (can't extract more leads without new resources)
- Quality and economics are optimized
- TAM is starting to feel constrained in current niche
- Cash reserves support investment in a new channel without killing the core

**What to do:**
- Add a second lead channel (usually cold outreach or paid ads if warm + organic are maxed)
- Consider a new offer (expansion of niche or adjacent offer to same ICP)
- Build the founding team to support the new initiative without degrading the core

**Trap to avoid:** Launching "New" before the core is truly optimized. The new initiative starves the core and fails.

## The Hormozi Scale Ladder

Revenue milestones and what typically unlocks each stage:

| Stage | Revenue | Primary Lever | Typical Constraint |
|---|---|---|---|
| $0–$10k/month | Pre-revenue | Warm outreach | Offer clarity + execution |
| $10k–$50k/month | Early traction | Content + warm | Closing + delivery quality |
| $50k–$200k/month | Growth | Cold outreach + SOPs | Founder bottleneck |
| $200k–$1M/month | Scaling | Paid ads + hiring | Systems + team |
| $1M–$5M/month | Scaling + leverage | New offers or verticals | Leadership + capital |
| $5M+/month | Portfolio | M&A or multiple brands | Organizational design |

## Applying the Scale Sequence to Lead Channels

The same More → Better → New logic applies within each channel:

### Warm Outreach
- **More:** Increase daily touches from 5 → 10 → 20
- **Better:** Improve setter script, personalization, follow-up cadence
- **New:** Add a second warm channel (LinkedIn + SMS)

### Content Machine
- **More:** Post daily on one platform before adding a second
- **Better:** Improve hook quality, repurpose top performers, A/B test CTAs
- **New:** Add a second platform or format once the first is optimized

### Cold Outreach
- **More:** Scale from 20 → 50 → 100 touches/day
- **Better:** Improve personalization, test subject lines, refine follow-up
- **New:** Add a second cold channel (email + LinkedIn + SMS)

### Paid Ads
- **More:** Increase budget 2x every 48–72 hours while ROAS holds
- **Better:** Optimize creative, landing page, offer positioning
- **New:** Add a second ad platform or test new audience segments

## Scale Sequence Decision Tree

```
Is volume consistent?
  NO → Phase 1: Add more volume. Stop here.
  YES ↓

Is quality / conversion optimized?
  NO → Phase 2: Improve what's working. Stop here.
  YES ↓

Is the current channel maxed?
  NO → Phase 1 of current channel: Add more volume.
  YES → Phase 3: Add new channel / offer.
```

Run this decision tree every 90 days. Never skip a phase.
