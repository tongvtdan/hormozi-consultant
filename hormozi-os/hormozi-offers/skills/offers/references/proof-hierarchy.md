# Proof Hierarchy — Reference
**Source: $100M Proof Checklist — Alex Hormozi (2024)**

> "Everyone can make the same promise, but no one can have the same proof."
> "Proof is the only thing that separates you from everyone else making the same claim."

---

## The 13-Axis Framework (Full Detail)

### Axis 1 — Format
| Level | Description | How to Get It |
|---|---|---|
| In-person | Client physically present; undeniable | Events, live demos, in-person testimonials |
| Virtual (live) | Live video call; can't be faked easily | Zoom testimonial calls, live case studies |
| Screenshot/image | Easy to screenshot; lower credibility | Default baseline |

### Axis 2 — Timing
| Level | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| Live | Cannot be edited or staged; highest trust |
| Recorded | Can be edited; reduced trust but still powerful |

### Axis 3 — Processing
| Level | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| Raw (unedited) | Feels authentic; imperfections = credibility |
| Produced (edited) | Polished = looks staged; reduces perceived authenticity |

### Axis 4 — Method
| Level | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| Show (demonstrate, screen share, data) | Irrefutable; seeing = believing |
| Tell (describe in words) | Can be fabricated; requires more trust leap |

### Axis 5 — Source
| Level | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| Others (customers, third parties) | No incentive to lie; highest credibility |
| You | Self-interested; needs more corroboration |

### Axis 6 — Relevance
| Level | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| Identical ICP | "That's exactly me" — maximum belief transfer |
| Similar ICP | "That's close to me" — high transfer |
| Generic | "That's someone else" — low transfer |

### Axis 7 — Specificity
| Level | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| Named, personal | Real person = real result = believable |
| Anonymous | Could be invented; lower credibility |

### Axis 8 — Magnitude
| Level | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| Big results | Dramatic transformation = powerful proof |
| Small results | Still valid; shows it works |
| No results | Absence of proof ≠ proof of absence, but useless |

### Axis 9 — Recency
| Level | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| Newer (< 6 months) | Market conditions are current; still applicable |
| Older (> 12 months) | Market may have changed; reduced relevance |

### Axis 10 — Volume
| Level | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| More proof (10+) | Pattern of success = systematic; not lucky |
| Less proof (1–3) | Could be cherry-picked; needs qualification |

### Axis 11 — Validation
| Level | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| Third-party verified | External audit = unbiased; maximum credibility |
| Self-reported | Could be inflated; needs supporting evidence |

### Axis 12 — Quantification
| Level | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| Specific numbers | "Revenue grew from $47K to $213K in 90 days" |
| Vague language | "Revenue grew a lot" — meaningless |

### Axis 13 — Accessibility
| Level | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| Metaphors/analogies | Brain processes faster; sticks in memory |
| Technical jargon | Creates distance; reduces understanding |

## Proof Acquisition Plan (by Stage)

### Stage 1 — Zero Social Proof
- Offer at cost or free to 3–5 ideal customers
- Get results, capture testimonials immediately
- Video > written; named > anonymous; numbers > vague

### Stage 2 — Some Proof
- Film client calls (with permission); use raw clips
- Request screen shares of results (dashboards, analytics, before/after)
- Get third-party validation where possible (accountants, doctors, verifiers)

### Stage 3 — Proof Stack
- Build a case study library organized by ICP type
- Create a results page with diverse proof across all 13 axes
- Add live proof elements (live client calls, public dashboard, community wins)

## Proof Sequence for Maximum Impact

Order your proof presentation for compounding credibility:

1. **Volume signal** — "Over [X] clients have used this" (establishes pattern)
2. **Most identical ICP case study** — "Here's someone exactly like you"
3. **Quantified result with timeline** — specific numbers + specific timeframe
4. **Third-party verification** — external validator or platform data
5. **Raw testimonial clip** — unpolished, authentic, live or recorded
6. **Before/after contrast** — visual proof of transformation
7. **Guarantee** — removes remaining risk after proof has done its job
