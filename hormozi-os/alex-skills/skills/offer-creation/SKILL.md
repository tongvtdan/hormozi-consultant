# The Offer Creation Framework

**Philosophy:** "Good products sell. Great offers print money."

## Trigger Conditions

Activate when the user has a problem to solve or a product to sell and needs a complete, conversion-ready offer structure.

## Execution Protocol

1. Confirm the core problem being solved if not specified.
2. Build all 5 offer components below.
3. Output structured like a landing page — not a business plan.
4. Each component must be specific and actionable, not generic.

## The 5-Component Offer Stack

### Component 1: Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)
Define with precision:
- **Who they are**: Role, industry, company size, or demographic
- **What they desperately want**: The dream outcome
- **What they've already tried**: Past failed solutions
- **What they fear**: The nightmare scenario if they don't solve this
- **What makes them qualified**: They have urgency + budget + authority

### Component 2: Value Proposition
One sentence. Fill in this template:
> "We help [ICP] go from [painful before state] to [desired after state] in [timeframe] without [biggest objection]."

Then expand with:
- Primary outcome (what they get)
- Secondary outcomes (bonuses / side benefits)
- Speed of delivery
- Ease or simplicity

### Component 3: Pricing Architecture (3 Tiers)

| Tier    | Price Point | What's Included             | Best For               |
|---------|-------------|-----------------------------|-----------------------|
| Entry   | $[low]      | Core result only            | Low-risk trial buyers |
| Core    | $[mid]      | Full solution + support     | Primary buyers        |
| Premium | $[high]     | Done-for-you + white-glove  | High-LTV clients      |

Notes on pricing:
- Price to value, not to cost
- Anchor the premium tier first (makes core look like a deal)
- Entry tier should generate momentum, not profit

### Component 4: Guarantee + Risk Reversal
Choose from these guarantee types and build the language:
- **Outcome guarantee**: "Get [result] in [time] or we [refund/work for free]"
- **Effort guarantee**: "Follow the process, or we'll keep working until you do"
- **Conditional guarantee**: "If you [do X], you'll get [Y] — guaranteed"

Write the guarantee headline and supporting copy.

### Component 5: Competitive Differentiation
Answer why this offer beats every alternative:
- vs. DIY: [reason]
- vs. Competitor A: [reason]
- vs. Doing nothing: [cost of inaction]

State the **Unfair Advantage** — the one thing competitors cannot copy quickly.

## Output Format

Structure the output exactly like a landing page:

```
## HERO SECTION
Headline: [Bold promise]
Subheadline: [Who it's for + mechanism]

## WHO THIS IS FOR
[ICP description — 3 bullet points]

## WHAT YOU GET
[Value prop — primary + secondary outcomes]

## PRICING
[3-tier table]

## GUARANTEE
[Guarantee headline + 2-sentence description]

## WHY US (NOT THEM)
[3-point differentiation]
```

## Quality Standard

Every element must be specific. "We help businesses grow" fails. "We help B2B SaaS founders under $5M ARR add $50K MRR in 90 days without hiring a sales team" passes.
