# The Viral Content Engine

**Philosophy:** "Content is the new distribution."

## Trigger Conditions

Activate when the user wants to build a repeatable content engine that generates organic reach, engagement, and conversions — for any niche or platform.

## Execution Protocol

1. Confirm the niche and primary platform(s) if not specified.
2. Generate 20 high-converting hooks tailored to that niche.
3. Define 10 content formats with rationale.
4. Map the emotional triggers that drive sharing behavior.
5. Explain the shareability mechanics — why audiences will distribute this content.
6. Output as a repeatable framework, not one-time ideas.

## The Viral Content Architecture

### Module 1: The Hook Arsenal (20 Hooks)

Hooks must do one of three things in under 3 seconds:
- **Interrupt** a scroll (pattern disrupt)
- **Promise** a specific outcome (value signal)
- **Create tension** that demands resolution (curiosity gap)

Hook categories to cover:
- Contrarian hook: "Everyone says [X]. They're wrong."
- Outcome hook: "How I [result] in [timeframe] without [sacrifice]"
- Pain hook: "[Specific painful situation]? This is why."
- Curiosity hook: "Most [audience] have no idea that [surprising fact]"
- List hook: "X things [audience] wish they knew before [event]"
- Story hook: "In [year], I [made massive mistake]. Here's what I learned."
- Warning hook: "Stop doing [common thing]. Here's what it's costing you."

Generate 20 hooks specific to the user's niche — each one ready to copy-paste.

### Module 3: Emotional Trigger Map

Viral content exploits one or more of these 7 triggers:

| Trigger     | What It Activates          | Content Mechanic                         |
|-------------|----------------------------|------------------------------------------|
| Fear        | Loss aversion              | "What you're losing by not doing X"      |
| Status      | Social signaling           | "What top [audience] know that you don't"|
| Curiosity   | Open loop compulsion       | "The real reason [X] happens"            |
| Anger       | Moral outrage              | "Why [industry/system] is broken"        |
| Hope        | Aspiration                 | "What's possible for [audience] in [Y]"  |
| Belonging   | Tribal identity            | "Signs you're a real [identity]"         |
| Surprise    | Pattern interruption       | "Nobody talks about [counterintuitive X]"|

For the user's niche, identify the top 3 most powerful triggers and match them to specific hooks and formats.

### Module 4: Shareability Mechanics

Content gets shared when it makes the sharer look good, feel something, or gives them a tool to use. 

For the user's content, answer:
- **Identity share**: "Sharing this makes me look [smart/caring/innovative] because..."
- **Tool share**: "I'd share this because it's useful for [specific situation]"
- **Emotional share**: "This made me feel [X] and I want others to feel it"
- **Social currency**: "Sharing this signals I'm [part of this movement/smarter than average]"

## Quality Standard

Every hook must name a specific audience, specific outcome, or specific pain — not broad concepts. "5 things entrepreneurs wish they knew" is weak. "5 things $1M/yr consultants do that freelancers think are crazy" is strong.
