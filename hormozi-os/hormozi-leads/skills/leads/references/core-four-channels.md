## The Mandatory Sequencing Rule

```
Warm Outreach → Content Machine → Cold Outreach → Paid Ads
```

**Why this order is non-negotiable:**

| If you skip… | The cost is… |
|---|---|
| Warm before Cold | You discover the script doesn't work on strangers — after burning 500 cold touches |
| Organic before Paid | You pay to learn your offer doesn't convert |
| Warm before Paid | You spend money to find out your ICP is wrong |

The sequence de-risks each stage. Every channel funds the next.

## Channel 1 — Warm Outreach

**Core principle:** You are not selling. You are opening a conversation to check if they have the problem.

### Who is a warm contact?

- Former/current clients
- Referrals (from existing clients)
- Past professional acquaintances
- Personal network with buying power
- Communities you're already a member of
- Past leads who didn't close

### Daily Execution Protocol

| Day | Action |
|---|---|
| Mon | 10 new warm openers sent |
| Tue–Thu | Follow up on non-responders; book from responders |
| Fri | Review conversation list; advance open threads |
| Ongoing | One new referral request per closed conversation |

**Daily target:** 10+ genuine warm conversations initiated

### The Warm Outreach Setter Script (ACQ Closer Handbook)

**Step 1 — Open (never pitch)**
```
"Hey [Name], how have ya been?"
```
- Wait for their response before proceeding
- Reference something genuine (their post, a shared connection, a recent event in their life)

**Step 2 — Context**
```
"[Personalized observation — their business, their situation, something you noticed]"
```
Examples:
- "I saw you posted about struggling with [specific issue]"
- "Congrats on the [promotion / launch / milestone]"
- "I remember you were dealing with [X problem] last time we talked"

**Step 3 — Discovery Question**
```
"Are you still dealing with [specific problem you solve]?"
```
- Must be a yes/no question
- Must reference a real pain, not a feature
- If yes → move to Step 4. If no → ask for a referral.

**Step 4 — Bridge (social proof before pitch)**
```
"I've been helping [2-3 similar people/businesses] achieve [specific result] in [specific timeframe]."
```
- Use identical ICP examples only — "someone exactly like you"
- Include real numbers: "from $40k to $90k/month in 11 weeks"

**Step 5 — CTA (permission to have one conversation)**
```
"Would 15 minutes make sense to see if it applies to your situation?"
```
- Not: "Would you like to buy?"
- Not: "Can I show you something?"
- Frame as a diagnostic, not a pitch

### Follow-Up Rules

| Touch | Timing | Message |
|---|---|---|
| Touch 1 | Day 0 | Original opener |
| Touch 2 | Day 2 | "Just circling back on this — still relevant?" |
| Touch 3 | Day 5 | Different angle, same question |
| Touch 4 (final) | Day 7 | Breakup: "Totally get if timing isn't right — want me to reach back out in [90 days]?" |

**Never pitch on Touch 1. Never give up after Touch 1.**

### Referral Ask (after every closed conversation, win or lose)

```
"Who else do you know dealing with [specific problem] that I might be able to help?"
```

A warm referral costs $0 and converts at 5–10x higher than cold.

## Channel 2 — Content Machine

**Core principle:** Content is the only lead gen channel that compounds over time without additional spend.

### Platform Activation Priority

Activate one primary platform first. Master it before adding a second.

| Platform | ICP | Content Type | Frequency | Goal |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| TikTok / Reels / Shorts | B2C, under 40 | 30–90 sec — hook + lesson + CTA | Daily | Organic reach, top-of-funnel |
| YouTube | B2B or B2C seeking depth | 10–20 min — case study / deep dive | Weekly | Authority, SEO, long-form trust |
| LinkedIn | B2B decision makers | 300–800 words — framework or story | 3×/week | B2B trust + professional credibility |
| X (Twitter) | Founders, builders, thinkers | Thread or sharp insight | Daily | Distribution, engagement |
| Newsletter | All ICPs with email | 500–1,500 words — value-first email | Weekly | Nurture, relationship depth |
| Skool / Discord | Community-driven ICPs | Discussion + AMAs | Daily | Community, high-engagement proof |

### The Content Flywheel

```
POST content
  → Organic engagement
  → Embedded lead magnet CTA
  → Contact captured (email / DM / form)
  → Nurture sequence
  → Offer presented
  → Client converts
  → Result produced
  → Case study content
  → Back to POST
```

The flywheel accelerates because case studies become the next post's hook — which attracts identical ICPs.

### Content Pillar Framework (3-pillar rule)

Build all content around 3 pillars that map to the buyer's journey:

| Pillar | Purpose | Example Formats |
|---|---|---|
| **Problem Awareness** | Make them realize the pain is real and solvable | "The #1 reason [ICP] can't [get result]" |
| **Solution Education** | Show them your framework/method works | Case studies, before/after, step-by-step |
| **Proof & Belief** | Transfer certainty that it works for them | Testimonials, results, named client wins |

**Rule:** Never let two consecutive posts be from the same pillar.

### The Hook Formula (for every post)

Every piece of content must have a hook in the first line or first 3 seconds:
```
[Pattern interrupt] + [Specific tension or promise] + [Implicit pull to keep consuming]
```

See `references/content-machine.md` for the full hook library.

### Lead Magnet Integration

Every post must contain exactly **one** CTA pointing to one lead magnet. No more. No exceptions.

> "Post content → CTA to lead magnet → collect contact → offer core product"

The lead magnet acts as the flywheel's collection mechanism.

## Channel 3 — Cold Outreach

**Gate criteria — both must be met before activating:**
- [ ] ≥ 5 paying warm customers (offer is validated)
- [ ] Warm outreach setter script is proven to convert

**Why:** Cold outreach with an unproven offer = expensive rejection data.

### Cold vs Warm Outreach — The Only Difference

Same script. Same structure. Same steps.

The only difference: **research required per contact.**

Cold requires minimum one personalized detail per message — something that proves you are not sending mass spam:
- A specific post they wrote
- A product/service they offer
- A result or milestone you observed
- A mutual connection or context

Volume target: 50–100 personalized touches per day  
Follow-up cadence: 5–7 touches over 14 days before marking cold  

### Sourcing Cold Lists

| Method | Quality | Speed |
|---|---|---|
| LinkedIn Sales Navigator | High | Medium |
| Apollo.io | High | Fast |
| Manual community scraping | Very high | Slow |
| Conference attendee lists | Very high | Slow |
| Referral seeding | Highest | Slow |

**Rule:** Own your list. Never rent attention you can't contact again.

## Channel 4 — Paid Ads

**Gate criteria — ALL must be met before spending a dollar:**

- [ ] LTV:CAC ratio confirmed > 3:1 (audit via hormozi-scale)
- [ ] Offer validated warm (≥ 10 paying customers from warm outreach)
- [ ] Lead magnet tested organically and converting
- [ ] Closing rate on qualified calls ≥ 20%

**Why:** Paid ads amplify what already works. They do not fix what doesn't.

### The Ad Creative Formula

```
Hook (from Workflow 4 — Hook Engine)
  → Case study proof (identical ICP — named, specific result, specific timeframe)
  → Lead magnet CTA (single, low-friction next step)
  → One action: click to landing page or DM to get the magnet
```

### Ad Testing Protocol

1. **Test hooks first** — 3–5 variations, same creative body. Kill losers at 500 impressions.
2. **Test offer/body copy** — once winning hook found
3. **Test landing page** — once ad copy converts
4. **Scale winner** — 2x budget every 48–72 hours while ROAS holds

### Retargeting Ladder

```
Cold audience → Engagement audience → Lead magnet audience → Offer audience
```

Each rung is warmer. Each rung gets a more direct offer.

## Channel ROI Reality Check

| Channel | Speed to First Lead | Cost | Compounding? | Scales to? |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Warm Outreach | Hours | $0 | No | ~$300k–$1M/yr (effort-bound) |
| Content Machine | Weeks/months | $0 (time) | Yes — exponentially | Unlimited |
| Cold Outreach | 24–48 hours | Low | Linearly | $1M–$5M/yr |
| Paid Ads | Minutes | High | No — pay-to-play | Unlimited (if LTV > CAC) |

**Bottom line:** Warm funds the business short-term. Content owns the long-term. Cold bridges the middle. Paid multiplies what works.
