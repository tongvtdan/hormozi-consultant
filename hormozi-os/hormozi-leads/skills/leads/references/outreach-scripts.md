# Outreach Scripts — Reference
**Source: $100M Leads + ACQ Closer Handbook — Alex Hormozi**

> "The goal of the first message is to get a response, not to make a sale."
> "Permission + interest + question = conversation."

---

## Universal Principles (Apply to All Scripts)

1. **Never pitch on first contact** — ask a question, open a thread
2. **End every message with a yes/no question** — makes response effortless
3. **Reference something specific and real** — proves you're not spamming
4. **Address pain, not features** — nobody cares what you do; they care what problem you solve
5. **One CTA per message** — multiple asks = zero actions taken
6. **Follow up 3–7 times before marking cold** — most sales happen on touch 3–5

## Script Library 1 — Warm Outreach (Primary)

### The Standard Setter Script (ACQ Closer Handbook)

**Message 1 — The Opener**
```
"Hey [Name] — how have ya been? [Personalized observation — their recent post / shared context / life event]"
```

Wait for response before proceeding.

**Message 2 — Discovery**
```
"Quick question — are you still dealing with [specific problem you solve]?"
```

If YES:

**Message 3 — Bridge + CTA**
```
"Got it. I've been helping [similar businesses/people] go from [specific before state] to [specific after state] in [timeframe].

Would it make sense to jump on a 15-minute call to see if there's something similar we could do for you?"
```

If NO:

**Referral Pivot**
```
"Makes sense! Who else do you know dealing with [problem]? Would love to help someone you trust."
```

### Warm Outreach — Referral Ask (Post-Conversation)

**After any call — win or loss:**
```
"Hey [Name] — really enjoyed our conversation. One quick ask: who else do you know dealing with [specific problem]? 

I'd love an introduction if you think they'd benefit from what we talked about."
```

**Text/DM variant:**
```
"Quick ask — who else in your network is dealing with [problem]? Happy to give them the same 15 minutes."
```

---

### Warm Outreach — Reconnect (Lapsed Relationship)

```
"Hey [Name] — it's been a while. Hope things are great on your end.

I saw [specific thing — their post, their business update, something real].

Quick question: are you still dealing with [specific pain]? I've been helping people in [their situation] with [specific outcome] lately and thought of you."
```

## Script Library 2 — Cold Outreach

**Gate:** Only activate after ≥5 paying warm customers + proven warm script.

### Cold Email — Standard (B2B)

**Subject:** [Specific observation about their business]

```
Hi [First name],

[One specific observation about their business — something real you researched]

I've helped [2-3 businesses exactly like theirs] achieve [specific result] in [specific timeframe].

Quick question: is [specific problem] currently something you're working on solving?

[Your name]
```

**Example (agency version):**
```
Hi Sarah,

Noticed your agency has strong case studies but no lead magnet on the site — your exit rate is probably high.

I've helped 3 boutique agencies under 10 people go from inconsistent referrals to 15+ qualified leads/month in 60 days, without hiring a sales team.

Quick question: is lead gen currently your biggest constraint to growth?

Dan
```

---

### Cold LinkedIn DM — Sequence

**Message 1 — Connection request note:**
```
"Hi [Name] — [Personalized hook: saw your post on X / noticed your company does Y / we share Z in common]. Would love to connect."
```

**Message 2 — 24 hours after accepting:**
```
"Thanks for connecting [Name]. [Reference to their work — genuine observation]. 

Quick question: are you currently working on solving [specific pain]?"
```

**Message 3 — 3 days later (if no response):**
```
"Hey [Name] — wanted to follow up on my question. [Restate specific pain + fresh observation].

Happy to share what's been working for others in your space — would a 10-minute call make sense?"
```

**Message 4 — 7 days later (if no response):**
```
"Last follow-up from me, [Name]. If the timing isn't right, totally get it.

If [problem] ever becomes a priority, happy to reconnect. Have a great [day/week]."
```

---

### Cold SMS / DM — Short Form

```
"Hey [Name] — [quick personalized hook]. 

Quick question: is [problem] currently something you're actively trying to solve?

[Your name] — [one-line descriptor: e.g., 'I work with B2B agencies on lead gen']"
```

## Script Library 3 — Referral Outreach

### Inbound Referral (Warm Introduction)

When a referral comes in through someone you know:

```
"Hey [Name] — [Mutual contact] mentioned you might be dealing with [problem]. 

I've helped [similar people] achieve [specific result] — wanted to reach out directly.

Would a quick 15-minute call be worth it to see if there's anything here?"
```

### Cold Referral Request (to existing clients)

```
"[Name] — quick favor. Who in your network is dealing with [problem right now]?

If you think of someone, I'd love a quick intro. I'll take care of the rest."
```

## Script Library 4 — Re-Engagement (Dead Leads)

### 90-Day Lapse

```
"Hey [Name] — it's been a few months. Wanted to check back in.

[New development: recent result / new offer element / relevant insight]

Is [problem] still something you're working on?"
```

### "Breakup" Message (After 4+ No-Responses)

```
"Hey [Name] — totally understand if the timing isn't right.

I'll leave you alone from here. If [problem] ever becomes a priority, I'm here.

Wishing you all the best regardless."
```

Why the breakup message works: removes pressure, sometimes triggers response, ends the sequence cleanly.

## Script Personalization Tiers

| Tier | Personalization Required | Volume/Day | Conversion Rate |
|---|---|---|---|
| Tier 1 (Warm) | Deep — knows the person | 10 | 20–40% to call |
| Tier 2 (Referred cold) | Medium — mutual context | 20 | 10–25% to call |
| Tier 3 (Researched cold) | Specific — one real detail | 50–100 | 3–8% to call |
| Tier 4 (Mass cold) | Template only | 200+ | < 1% to call (do not use) |

**Rule:** Never use Tier 4. The response rate is so low it poisons the sender reputation and signals you're not a serious operator.

## Call Booking CTA Variations

| Context | CTA |
|---|---|
| Warm lead, clear yes | "Are you free [Tuesday at 2pm or Thursday at 10am]?" |
| Cold lead, less certainty | "Would a 15-minute call make sense to see if it applies?" |
| High-ticket | "Would it be worth 20 minutes to map out exactly what that would look like for you?" |
| Low-friction (community) | "Drop a 'yes' below and I'll send you the link." |
| Text/SMS | "Reply 'yes' and I'll send you the calendar link." |

**Rule:** Always propose 2 specific times OR a calendar link. "Let me know when you're free" = no booking.

## Message Length Guidelines

| Channel | Ideal Length | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Cold email | 4–6 sentences | Long emails = instant delete |
| LinkedIn DM | 2–3 sentences | Mobile-first; short = higher reply rate |
| SMS | 1–3 sentences | 160 character sweet spot |
| Warm text | 3–5 sentences | Casual, not formal |
| Warm email | 5–8 sentences | Familiar context = can say a bit more |

**Formatting rule:** No long paragraphs. One idea per line. White space signals respect for their time.
