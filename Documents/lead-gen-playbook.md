## The Lead Gen Hierarchy (Source-True)

> "It's hard to be poor with leads bangin' down your door." — Hormozi family jingle

A lead = a person you can contact.
An **engaged lead** = a person who shows interest in the stuff you sell.
**Engaged leads are the true output of advertising.**

Always activate in this sequence. Later channels amplify earlier ones.

```
1. Warm Outreach    → Fastest path to first dollar
2. Content Machine  → Builds compounding authority
3. Cold Outreach    → Scalable when offer is proven
4. Paid Ads         → Amplifies what already converts
```

> "All else being equal... when you double your leads, you double your business."

## The Problem-Solution Cycle (Lead Magnet Design)

Every problem has a solution. Every solution reveals more problems.
The lead magnet solves the NARROW problem. Your core offer solves the NEXT problem.

**Example (from book):** Homeowners want to sell their home (broad) → "What's my home worth?" free report (narrow) → Now they still need to actually sell it → Core offer: full listing service.

---

## 7 Steps to Create an Effective Lead Magnet (from Book)

**Step 1 — Pick the problem and the person**
Narrow and meaningful. After solving it, the next problem must be solvable by your core offer.

**Step 2 — Figure out how to solve it**
Three types: (1) Awareness magnet — reveals a problem they don't know they have; (2) Trial/sample — solves a recurring problem for a short time; (3) One step in multi-step process — gives one piece, rest requires core offer

**Step 3 — Figure out how to deliver it**
Apply the Delivery Cube: 1-on-1/group/one-to-many · DIY/DWY/DFY · Live/recorded/written · 24/7 vs scheduled

**Step 4 — Test the name** (use MAGIC formula)
Example: "FREE Case Study: How We Added 213 Members and $112,000 in Revenue to a Small Gym in San Diego"

**Step 5 — Make it easy to consume**
Lower effort = higher perceived value. Short, visual, actionable.

**Step 6 — Make it darn good**
"Make your lead magnet so insanely good people will feel stupid saying no."

**Step 7 — Make it easy for them to tell you they want more**
One clear CTA to the next step. No ambiguity.

## 6 Lead Magnet Formats

| Format | Best For | Effort Score |
|---|---|---|
| **Checklist** | Complex processes, compliance, launch prep | Very Low |
| **Template** | Anything with repeatable structure | Very Low |
| **Tool/Calculator** | Quantifiable outcomes (ROI, score, savings) | Low |
| **Case Study** | Proving results for an identical ICP | Low-Medium |
| **Mini-Course (3–5 lessons)** | Education-heavy offers | Medium |
| **Community Access (Free Tier)** | Network-effect businesses | Medium |

> "Tools & checklists are better than additional trainings — effort & time are lower, so value is higher."

## Channel 1: Warm Outreach (Full Script — from ACQ Handbook)

### Setter Script Template

```
Opening (personalized):
"Hey [Name] — I'm [Name] from [Company]. How have ya been?"

After pleasantries:
"I'm just calling because you [responded to / were referred by / I noticed] 
[source] about potentially getting help with [problem you solve].
Is now a bad time?"

Research demonstration:
"I've done a little research on [Company] — I'm on your website right now 
and it looks like you're doing some really great work in [niche].
In your own words, can you give me a quick 20-30 second overview
of what you do and what makes you unique?"

Gap identification:
"What is the big goal? And how soon are you looking to do this by?"

Bridge:
"I appreciate you sharing this. Would you mind if I share my .02?"
"We see this all the time. [Briefly overview why current approach isn't working
and hint at the solution]. What would your business look like if [specific 
improvement]?"

CTA (within 36 hours):
"I have a slot at [X] tomorrow or [X] the day after — which works better?"
```

**Rules:** Never pitch in first message · Always end with yes/no question · Follow up 3x over 7 days · Target 10+ warm conversations/day

## Channel 2: Content Machine

### Content Pillars (pick 2–3)

| Pillar | Example |
|---|---|
| Education | "How to Fill Your Calendar in 30 Days Without Cold Calling" |
| Mistakes | "Why Most Agency Owners Are Leaving 40% of Revenue on the Table" |
| Behind-the-scenes | "How We Went From $0 to $100K/Month — Here's the Actual P&L" |
| Contrarian | "Stop Focusing on Followers. Here's What Actually Makes Money." |
| Case Study | "How a 3-Location Gym Added $40K in 30 Days With One Offer Change" |
| Framework | "The Core Four Lead Sources (and Which to Start With)" |

### Posting Cadence

| Channel | Format | Minimum Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| TikTok / Reels / Shorts | 30–90 sec hook + lesson + CTA | Daily |
| YouTube | 10–20 min deep dive / case study | Weekly |
| LinkedIn / Newsletter | 300–800 word framework or story | 3× per week |
| X (Twitter) | Thread or single insight | Daily |
| Skool / Discord | Discussion prompt or AMA | Daily |

### The Content Flywheel

```
POST → Organic engagement → Lead magnet CTA → Contact captured
→ Nurture sequence → Offer presented → Client converts
→ Result achieved → Case study created → Back to POST
```

## Channel 3: Cold Outreach

Only activate after warm outreach has validated the offer.
Same script as warm outreach — personalized at scale.
Volume: 50–100 personalized touches per day.
Follow-up: 5–7 touches across 14 days before marking cold.

---

## Channel 4: Paid Ads

**Gate criteria before spending:**
- LTV:CAC ratio confirmed > 3:1
- Offer validated via warm outreach (≥ 10 paying customers)
- Lead magnet tested and converting organically
- Closing rate on qualified calls ≥ 20%

**Creative formula:** Hook → Case study/proof → Offer + lead magnet CTA → Single clear next step
