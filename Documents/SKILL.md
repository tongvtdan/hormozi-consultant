# Hormozi Offer OS

A complete AI co-founder toolkit operationalizing Alex Hormozi's $100M frameworks.
Built for solo founders and startup studios running multiple ventures simultaneously.

---

## Core Philosophy

> "Your offer isn't what you sell — it's how you package and frame the value."
> "Make the dream outcome obvious, the likelihood of success undeniable, and the effort feel invisible."

Five laws that govern every decision in this OS:

1. **Offer beats marketing** — Fix the offer before touching the ads
2. **Value = (Dream × Likelihood) / (Time × Effort)** — The Value Equation governs everything
3. **Niche down to dominate** — Charge more by serving fewer people better
4. **Risk reversal unlocks buying** — Whoever absorbs the risk controls the transaction
5. **Distribution is leverage** — No distribution = no business, regardless of offer quality

## Workflow 1: Value Equation Scorer {#workflow-1}

**When to use:** User has an existing offer and wants to know if it's good, or wants to improve it.

### Step 1 — Gather Offer Data

Ask (or infer from context):
- What is the offer exactly?
- Who is the ideal customer?
- What is the dream outcome promised?
- What does the customer have to do / sacrifice?
- How long until they see results?

### Step 2 — Score Using the 4-Part Value Equation

```
Value = (Dream Score × Likelihood Score) / (Time Score × Effort Score)
```

| Component | What It Measures | Scale |
|---|---|---|
| Dream Score | How desirable is the promised outcome? | 1–100 |
| Likelihood Score | How believable is success? | 1–100 |
| Time Score | How long until results? (higher = worse) | 0–1 |
| Effort Score | How hard does the customer have to work? (higher = worse) | 0–1 |

**Offer Score** = (Dream × Likelihood) / (Time × Effort)

Perfect score = 1,000,000 (Dream: 100, Likelihood: 100, Time: 0.1, Effort: 0.1)

### Step 3 — Output Format

Provide:
1. Score breakdown for all 4 components + overall Offer Score
2. #1 weakness and exact fix
3. 2 alternative offer structures with higher scores
4. Optional: rewrite the offer to approach score 1,000,000

**Read:** `references/value-equation-deep-dive.md` for advanced scoring calibration examples.

## Workflow 2: Grand Slam Offer Builder {#workflow-2}

**When to use:** User wants to create a new offer from scratch or reconstruct an existing one.

### The 6-Layer Grand Slam Offer Stack

Work through each layer in sequence:

**Layer 1 — Niche & ICP**
- Who is the most desperate buyer in this market?
- What is their #1 painful problem RIGHT NOW?
- What have they already tried that failed?

**Layer 2 — Dream Outcome**
- What does their life look like AFTER?
- Make it vivid, specific, and emotionally resonant
- Use "from X to Y in Z time" structure

**Layer 3 — Problem → Solution Matrix**
- List every obstacle between them and the dream outcome
- For each obstacle, create a solution/deliverable
- Stack deliverables into a bundle (each gets a standalone dollar value)

**Layer 4 — Pricing Architecture**

| Tier | Strategy |
|---|---|
| Value anchor | State the combined standalone value of all deliverables |
| Actual price | Set at 10x+ below stated value |
| Payment structure | One-time, payment plan, or performance-based |

**Layer 5 — Risk Reversal**
Choose one or stack multiples:
- Money-back guarantee (unconditional / conditional)
- "You don't pay until [result]" performance guarantee
- Free trial period
- "Keep the bonuses" guarantee
- Done-for-you guarantee (we do it if you can't)

**Layer 6 — Scarcity & Urgency**
- **Scarcity** = limited quantity (cohort size, spots, physical units)
- **Urgency** = limited time (deadline, bonus expiry, price increase)
- Must be REAL — manufactured scarcity destroys trust

**Output format:** Landing page structure with headline, subheadline, benefit bullets, value stack, price reveal, guarantee, and CTA.

**Read:** `references/grand-slam-offer-examples.md` for 5 worked examples across different niches.

## Workflow 3: Lead Magnet Generator {#workflow-3}

**When to use:** User wants more leads, a lead magnet, a content strategy, or an audience growth plan.

### The 4 Lead Sources (from $100M Leads)

```
Warm Outreach → Content Machine → Cold Outreach → Paid Ads
```

Always sequence in this order — warm before cold, organic before paid.

### Lead Magnet Design Framework

A lead magnet must solve ONE specific problem for ONE specific person and deliver IMMEDIATE value.

**The Lead Magnet Formula:**
```
[Specific outcome] for [specific ICP] in [specific timeframe] without [specific pain]
```

**6 Lead Magnet Formats:**
1. **Checklist** — "The 12-point offer audit every founder should run"
2. **Template** — Plug-and-play, reduces effort score to near zero
3. **Mini-course** (3–5 lessons) — Establishes authority fast
4. **Tool/calculator** — Interactive = high perceived value
5. **Case study** — "How [niche] went from X to Y in Z days"
6. **Community access** — Free tier of a paid community

### Content Machine (Core Four Channels)

| Channel | Format | Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| Short video (TikTok/Reels/Shorts) | Hook + lesson + CTA | Daily |
| Long video (YouTube) | Deep dive / case study | Weekly |
| Written (LinkedIn/Newsletter) | Framework or story | 3x/week |
| Community (Skool/Discord) | Discussion + AMA | Daily |

**The Content Flywheel:**
1. Post → Attracts leads organically
2. Lead magnet → Captures email / number
3. Nurture sequence → Builds trust
4. Offer → Converts to paying customer
5. Result → Case study → Back to step 1

**Read:** `references/lead-gen-playbook.md` for outreach scripts and channel-specific tactics.

## Workflow 4: Hooks & Headline Engine {#workflow-4}

**When to use:** User needs content hooks, ad headlines, email subject lines, or landing page copy.

### Hook Architecture

Every great hook has 3 components:
```
[Pattern interrupt] + [Specific promise or tension] + [Implicit "keep reading" pull]
```

### The 7 Hook Templates

1. **Contrast hook:** "Most [people] [common belief]. The top 1% [opposite truth]."
2. **Number hook:** "[Specific number] [outcome] in [specific timeframe] — here's exactly how"
3. **Mistake hook:** "The #1 mistake [ICP] make when [action] (and how to fix it in [time])"
4. **Secret hook:** "What [authority] never tells you about [topic]"
5. **Story hook:** "I [painful past state]. Today [transformed state]. Here's what changed."
6. **Question hook:** "What would you do if [scenario that forces the reader to imagine]?"
7. **Bold claim hook:** "[Counterintuitive statement that is provably true]"

### Headline Formula (Landing Pages / Ads)

```
[Action verb] + [Dream outcome] + [Timeframe] + [Without pain/objection]
```

Example: "Generate 50 qualified leads per week without cold calling or running ads."

### Output Format for Hook Requests

Always deliver:
- 5 hooks in different styles from the 7 templates above
- 1 headline using the formula
- 1 email subject line variant
- Emotional trigger used (fear, status, curiosity, greed, belonging)

## Workflow 5: Market Breakdown System {#workflow-5}

**When to use:** User is validating an idea, entering a new market, or doing competitive research.

### Market Analysis Framework

**Step 1 — TAM/SAM/SOM**
- **TAM** (Total Addressable Market) — Everyone who could ever buy this
- **SAM** (Serviceable Addressable Market) — Who you can realistically reach
- **SOM** (Serviceable Obtainable Market) — Who you'll capture in year 1-3

**Step 2 — Market Demand Signals**
Rate each on 1-10:
- Pain level: Is this problem causing real suffering?
- Purchasing power: Can they pay premium prices?
- Market accessibility: Can you reach them?
- Growth trajectory: Is this market expanding?

**Step 3 — Competition Analysis**
- Who are the top 3-5 players?
- What is their core offer?
- What complaints do customers have? (Check reviews, forums)
- Where is the gap?

**Step 4 — Monopoly Position (Thiel Logic)**
- What makes this defensible in 5 years?
- Network effects / switching costs / proprietary tech / brand?
- Are you competing or creating a new category?

**Output:** Structured table + 3 underserved opportunities + recommended entry wedge.

**Read:** `references/market-analysis-templates.md` for structured output templates.

## Workflow 6: Scale OS {#workflow-6}

**When to use:** User wants to scale an existing business, remove bottlenecks, or build systems.

### The Scale Sequence

```
Fix offer → Build acquisition → Automate fulfillment → Hire → Systematize → Scale
```

**Never skip steps.** Scaling a broken offer just amplifies the loss.

### 4-Layer Scale Audit

**Layer 1 — Offer Health Check** (run Workflow 1 first)
**Layer 2 — Acquisition Audit**
- Current CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)?
- LTV (Lifetime Value)?
- LTV:CAC ratio must be >3:1 to scale paid
**Layer 3 — Fulfillment Bottleneck**
- What breaks at 2x volume?
- What to automate vs delegate?
**Layer 4 — Team & Systems**
- First hire: replaces YOUR time in fulfillment
- Second hire: replaces YOUR time in sales
- Third hire: first manager

### Output Format

Execution roadmap with: 30-day / 90-day / 1-year milestones, automation priorities, hiring sequence, and top 3 bottlenecks to eliminate first.

## Output Principles (Apply to All Workflows)

1. **Be specific, never vague** — "within 14 days" not "quickly"
2. **Use numbers** — "92% success rate" beats "most customers succeed"
3. **Show the before/after** — Contrast sells
4. **Stack value visibly** — Line-item the value of every deliverable
5. **One CTA** — Never give the prospect two things to do
6. **Lead with the dream, close with the guarantee** — Emotion opens, logic justifies, fear stops → guarantee removes fear
