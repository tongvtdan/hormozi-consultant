# Hormozi Offer OS v2.0

A complete AI co-founder toolkit operationalizing Alex Hormozi's $100M frameworks.
Built from the source books, course checklists, and Acquisition.com playbooks.
Designed for solo founders and startup studios running multiple ventures simultaneously.

## Core Philosophy

> "Make people an offer so good they would feel stupid saying no." — Travis Jones (as cited by Hormozi)
> "The pain is the pitch. If you can articulate the pain a prospect is feeling accurately, they will almost always buy what you are offering."
> "Don't make me niche slap you."

### Five Laws That Govern Every Decision in This OS

1. **Offer beats marketing** — Fix the offer before touching the ads. A Grand Slam Offer at 22x better ROAS is documented in the source book.
2. **Value = (Dream × Likelihood) / (Time × Effort)** — The Value Equation governs everything
3. **Starving crowd > offer strength > persuasion skills** — Market selection is the highest-order lever
4. **Niche down 3–4 levels** — The same product sold to a specific avatar can be priced 100x higher
5. **Risk reversal unlocks buying** — Whoever absorbs the risk controls the transaction

## Workflow 1: Value Equation Scorer

**Source:** $100M Offers, Chapter 6 — "The Value Equation"

**Formula:** Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood) ÷ (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice)

**Perfect score = 1,000,000** (Dream: 100, Likelihood: 100, Time: 0.1, Effort: 0.1)

Scoring tiers: 1M+ = Grand Slam | 500k-999k = Elite | 100k-499k = Strong | 20k-99k = Decent | 5k-19k = Weak | <5k = Broken (do NOT run ads)

**4 Fastest Levers:** Add guarantee → Add DFY element → Add 7-day quick win → Niche down ICP

**Output:** Score report with component breakdown + weaknesses in priority order (denominator first) + exact fixes + improvement scenarios + bottom line

**See:** `value-equation-deep-dive.md` for 10 scored real-world examples

## Workflow 2: Grand Slam Offer Builder

**Source:** $100M Offers, Chapters 8–16 + 7 Course Checklists

**Definition:** "An offer you present to the marketplace that cannot be compared to any other product or service available..."

**8-Layer Stack:**
1. Market & Niche (4 criteria: Pain · Power · Targeting · Growing — niche 3-4 levels)
2. Dream Outcome ("From X to Y in Z time" + downstream benefits)
3. Problem → Solution Matrix (every obstacle + deliverable)
4. Delivery Cube (6 dimensions: Group · Effort DIY/DWY/DFY · Support · Consumption · Speed · 10x/1/10th test)
5. Trim & Stack (remove high-cost/low-value; stack with standalone $ values)
6. Pricing Architecture (value anchor >> actual price; 10x gap minimum)
7. Bonuses ("The Sizzle" — presented AFTER price; stack instead of discounts)
8. Risk Reversal (14 guarantee types) + Scarcity/Urgency (must be REAL)

**MAGIC Naming:** M=Magnetic reason · A=Avatar · G=Goal · I=Time interval · C=Container word

**Output:** Full landing page structure

**See:** `grand-slam-offer-examples.md` for 5 complete worked offers

## Workflow 3: Lead Magnet Generator

**Source:** $100M Leads + ACQ Closer Handbook

**Core Four (always this sequence):**
1. Warm Outreach → fastest to first dollar
2. Content Machine → compounding organic authority
3. Cold Outreach → scalable when offer is proven
4. Paid Ads → amplifies what converts (gate: LTV:CAC > 3:1)

**7-Step Lead Magnet:** Pick problem → Solve it (3 types) → Deliver it (Delivery Cube) → Name it (MAGIC) → Easy to consume → Make it darn good → Easy CTA to want more

**Output:** Lead magnet spec + MAGIC name + warm outreach script + 30-day content calendar + flywheel map

**See:** `lead-gen-playbook.md` for full scripts and channel tactics

## Workflow 5: Market Breakdown System

**Source:** $100M Offers, Chapter 4 — "A Starving Crowd"

**Rule:** Market > Offer > Persuasion. Niche pricing reality: same content = 100x price at Level 4 niche.

**4 Criteria (1-10 each):** Massive Pain · Purchasing Power · Easy to Target · Growing

**Output:** Market scorecard + Niche ladder + TAM/SAM/SOM + Competitive map + 3 underserved gaps + ICP card + Monopoly position

**See:** `market-analysis-templates.md` for structured templates

## Workflow 6: Scale OS

**Scale sequence (never skip):** Fix offer → Build acquisition → Automate fulfillment → Hire → Systematize → Scale

**4-Layer Audit:** Offer health (>100k score) → Acquisition (LTV:CAC >3:1; Hormozi avg 36:1) → Fulfillment bottleneck (what breaks at 2x?) → Hiring sequence (Fulfillment → Sales → Manager — never reverse)

**Output:** Top 3 bottlenecks by revenue impact + 30/90/365-day roadmap + automation priorities + hiring sequence

## Workflow 7: Proof Stack Builder

**Source:** $100M Proof Checklist — Alex Hormozi (2024)

> "Everyone can make the same promise, but no one can have the same proof."

**13-Axis Proof Hierarchy (most → least persuasive):**
In-person > Virtual | Live > Recorded | Raw > Processed | Show > Tell | Others > You | Identical ICP > Generic | Named > Anonymous | Big > Small | Newer > Older | More > Less | Third-party > None | With numbers > Without | Metaphors > Jargon

**Output:** Current proof rating on each axis + 3 biggest gaps + proof acquisition plan + assembled proof sequence

## Output Principles (Apply to ALL Workflows)

1. **Specific, never vague** — numbers always
2. **Show before/after** — painful current state → transformed future
3. **Stack value visibly** — line-item every deliverable with standalone $ values
4. **One CTA** — never two options at close
5. **Lead with dream, close with guarantee**
6. **Stack bonuses, not discounts**
7. **The pain is the pitch**
